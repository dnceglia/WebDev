Grand Slams

One of my interests growing up was playing tennis. During the year, I spent a good amount of time stringing tennis rackets and watching the major tennis tournaments known as the Grand Slams. Here's a brief introduction to them.

The Australian Open

The first major tennis tournament of the year is played in mid-January, "down-under", in Melbourne, Australia. This tournament has been played at the Melbourne Park facility for nearly 30 years. It is known as a "hard-court" tournament due to its Plexicushion surface. The event attracts the top players from around the world to compete for the almost $40 million USD prize purse that is split between participants.

The French Open

The second Grand Slam held at the end of May is the French Open, at Roland Garros. It is held in Paris in the Boulogne-Billancourt neighborhood which is surrounded by the Seine river. What's unique about the French Open is that it is the only major tournament played on red crushed brick. The surface, called a clay court, causes the ball to bounce higher than on hard or grass courts and requires the players to slide often when chasing down the ball.

Wimbledon

The third and oldest major tennis tournament is The Championships, Wimbledon held in July. The event is played at the All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club, and as the name implies, is played on grass. Odd features of this tournament is a stict "all-white clothing" dress code, no visible title sponsorship like at the other majors and of course, the Royal Box for the Royal Family.

The U.S. Open

The final grand slam of the tennis calendar occurs in Flushing Meadows, NY at the Billie Jean King Tennis Center. Originally, played on grass, the tournament was played on clay shortly before being changed to the hard court surface, DecoTurf. This hard court surface is fast and similar to the surface at Melbourne Park. The main event court is named Arthur Ashe Stadium and is the largest tennis related stadium in the world.
